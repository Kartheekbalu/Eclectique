Workshop on Generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAl)

Register Now

Welcome to the Workshop on Generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAl), where innovation meets intelligence! GenAl represents the cutting-edge fusion of Generative Models and Artificial Intelligence, paving the way for groundbreaking advancements in machine creativity. Join us as we embark on an enlightening journey into the realm of GenAl, exploring its potential to revolutionize various domains and drive innovation forward.

Projects

  • Random Image Generation: Unleashing the power of generative models to create diverse and unique images.
  • Conditional Image Generation: Harnessing GenAl to generate images based on specific conditions or criteria.
  • Image Reconstruction with VAEs: Utilizing Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) to reconstruct images from incomplete or corrupted data.
  • Conditional Image Generation for Image Recognition of Electrical Components: Leveraging GenAl for enhanced image recognition and analysis of electrical components.
  • Fault Detection in Power Systems: Applying GenAl techniques to detect and diagnose faults in power systems, improving reliability and efficiency.

Workshop Description

Project 1: Simple GAN for Random Image Generation

Basic GAN Architecture: Explanation of generator and discriminator components. Introduction to loss functions (e.g., binary cross-entropy). Implementing GAN Model in TensorFlow/PyTorch: Step-by-step code walkthrough. Training the model on a simple dataset (e.g., MNIST). Generating Random Images: Visualizing the generated images. Discussing challenges and potential improvements.

Project 2: Conditional Image Generation

Implementing a Conditional GAN: Modifying the GAN architecture for conditional generation. Coding exercises for conditional image synthesis. Training on a Labeled Dataset: Introduction to labeled datasets (e.g., CelebA). Fine-tuning the model for specific attribute conditioning. Generating Images Conditioned on Specific Attributes: Demonstrating the model's ability to generate images with desired attributes. Evaluating results and potential improvements.

Project 3: Image Reconstruction with VAEs

Implementing a VAE for Image Reconstruction: Coding a VAE model for image reconstruction. Analyzing reconstruction quality and limitations. Analyzing the Latent Space: Visualizing the learned latent space. Discussing the impact of latent variables on image generation.

Project 4: AI-Based Image Recognition for Electrical Components

Description: Develop an image recognition system using deep learning to automatically identify and classify various electrical components, including resistors, capacitors, and transistors, from images. Skills: Participants will gain expertise in computer vision, deep learning, and image processing through this project.

Project 5: Fault Detection in Power Systems

Description: Implement an AI-powered system for real-time fault detection in power distribution systems. Utilize machine learning algorithms, particularly deep learning techniques, to analyze sensor data. The goal is to identify anomalies that could indicate faults or abnormalities in the power system. Skills: Participants will develop skills in signal processing, machine learning, and gain insights into power systems through this project.
